# Coaching frameworks and questions

Reference library for [[skills/executive-coach/SKILL]] and [[L3_sops/executive-coaching-method]]. The top-rated, most-used executive coaching frameworks, kept only to the parts that help an AI coach. Cite when used.

## The one discipline above all: tame the advice monster

The Coaching Habit's core finding: leaders default to *telling*; coaching is *asking*. "Say less, ask more." Alfred's default stance ([[patterns/incisive-expert-communication]]) is to deliver the answer — in coach mode, **suppress the first answer, ask first, let the user reach it.** The reflex to advise is the thing to police.

## The 7 essential questions (Michael Bungay Stanier — verbatim)

Conversational engine. Use as needed, not in order. The AWE question is the workhorse — ask it 3–5x before moving on.

1. **Kickstart** — "What's on your mind?"
2. **AWE (And What Else?)** — "And what else?" *(the most useful; the first answer is rarely the only or best one)*
3. **Focus** — "What's the real challenge here for you?" *(the "for you" forces it from abstract to personal)*
4. **Foundation** — "What do you want?"
5. **Lazy** — "How can I help?" *(stops Alfred jumping to solve the wrong problem)*
6. **Strategic** — "If you're saying yes to this, what are you saying no to?"
7. **Learning** — "What was most useful to you?" *(closes the loop; cements the learning)*

## GROW — the structured session (most widely used model)

Translate ambition → measurable action. Best for short-horizon performance/decision coaching.

- **G — Goal.** What do you want from this conversation, and longer term? Make it specific.
- **R — Reality.** What's actually happening now? Independent estimate, not the user's framing. *(Alfred's verification spine lives here.)*
- **O — Options.** What could you do? Generate breadth before judging. Use AWE.
- **W — Way forward / Will.** What *will* you do, by when? Name the first reversible action.

## OSKAR — solution-focused, with scaling (complements GROW)

Best when the user is stuck on problems rather than outcomes.

- **O — Outcome.** Desired future state.
- **S — Scale.** "On 1–10, where are you now? What would +1 look like?" *(scaling questions surface progress and confidence cheaply — reuse for follow-ups.)*
- **K — Know-how.** What's already working / what resources exist?
- **A — Affirm & Action.** Affirm strengths, then the next step.
- **R — Review.** What's better since last time?

## Feedforward over feedback (Marshall Goldsmith — #1 ranked exec coach)

When the user wants to improve a behavior: don't relitigate the past (feedback breeds defensiveness). **Feedforward** = specific suggestions for the *future*. Pick one behavior to change, ask for forward-looking ideas, listen without rebutting. Goldsmith's standard: behavioral change must be **visible, measurable, and verified with stakeholders** — not self-assessed. Book: *What Got You Here Won't Get You There.*

## Coachability gate (Trillion Dollar Coach — Bill Campbell)

"Only coach the coachable." Traits: honesty, humility, willingness to persevere/work hard, openness to learning. If the user is defending instead of exploring, name it — that's the coaching moment. Other Campbell principles worth holding:

- Don't tell people what to do; offer stories, guide them to *their* best decision.
- Listen with full attention; ask questions to get to the *real* issue.
- Decide from **first principles** — the immutable truths of the situation.
- People-first, team-first. (Founder-relevant for [[pursuits/wanderland]].)

Book: *Trillion Dollar Coach* (Schmidt/Rosenberg/Eagle) — the Silicon Valley founder-coaching playbook.

## Immunity to Change (Kegan & Lahey) — for stuck, recurring patterns

When a commitment keeps failing despite real effort, the cause is usually a hidden **competing commitment**. Map: (1) improvement goal, (2) what you do/don't do against it, (3) the competing commitment that behavior protects, (4) the big assumption underneath. Surface the assumption; design a small safe test of it. Use only for genuinely recurring self-sabotage, not one-off decisions.

## Choosing a framework

| Situation | Use |
|---|---|
| Single decision or goal, want action | GROW |
| Stuck on problems, low momentum | OSKAR (scaling) |
| Wants to change a behavior/habit | Feedforward |
| Same failure keeps recurring | Immunity to Change |
| Any conversation, moment to moment | The 7 questions + AWE |
| User defending, not exploring | Name it (coachability) |
