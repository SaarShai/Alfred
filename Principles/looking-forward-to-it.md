# Looking forward to it

Frankl/paradoxical intention: welcome/invite what you avoid (overwhelm, sleepiness, failure) → it loses power. Load-bearing mechanism.

- **Basin:** H — Avoidance → invite (mechanism) · see [[Principles/network]]
- **Status:** LIVE · **sub-principle of [[Principles/enjoy]] (#1) and [[Principles/replace]]** (user-stated 2026-05-16)
- **Source:** [[raw/2026-05-12-gemini-spark-google-doc]] (immutable; verbatim text there)

## Role

Not a top-bunch peer — the operational *child* of two live parents:

- under **[[Principles/replace]]** (basin B): instead of fighting the dread, switch the framing to anticipation. Look-forward-to-it is replace applied to avoidance.
- under **[[Principles/enjoy]]** (#1, basin F): the way #1 actually reaches an un-fun task — invited-hard becomes enjoyed.

Path: **Replace (B) → look-forward-to-it (invite) → Enjoy (F)**. This is why #1↔#2 only *feels* like a contradiction: the bridge is a sub-principle of #1 the user under-reaches for, not a missing fifth principle.

## Connects to
