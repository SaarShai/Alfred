# Emergency Batch

The triage subset — distinct from the everyday [[Principles/top-bunch]]. What pulls the user *out* of a bad moment, not what runs the good day.

## Retrieval

- **On-request only. No watcher** (user-stated 2026-05-16). Do not auto-detect or interrupt.
- Trigger: the user explicitly asks for "help" or to "bring up the batch" / "the emergency batch". When they do, present this page's list first, plainly.
- Known but unscoped: the user sometimes can't self-detect the emergency state, or forgets to check this batch. A proactive detector was explicitly deferred — do not build it until the user reopens that.

## The batch (user-stated 2026-05-16)

**Run as a checklist, in order** (user-stated 2026-05-16) — not a ranked grab-bag. Sequence: defuse the false thought (1) → recommit to the pledge (2) → amplify (3). Present it that way: step through, don't offer a menu.

1. **"Is it true?!"** ([[Principles/is-it-true]]) — you're a negatively-biased judge; test the thought before believing it; "can't" → "why not?!"
2. **The pledge to enjoy no matter what** ([[Principles/enjoy]] / [[Principles/happy-anyway]]) — a *pledge*, not a preference. Stronger than [[Principles/choose]]: a standing commitment to enjoy regardless of conditions.
3. **25% more** ([[Principles/25-percent-more]]) — you can always dial *now* 25% more enjoyable; turn it to 11, then 20.

## Exit condition

Felt, not articulable — "I just know" (user-stated 2026-05-16). No metric, no verification step. When presenting the batch: step through 1→3, then stop when the user says they're good. Do not test it, do not push past it, do not ask them to justify the exit.
