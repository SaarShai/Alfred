# Active Top Bunch

## 2026-05-15 (user-stated)

1. **Enjoyment over management** ([[Principles/enjoy]]) — things must be enjoyable; find joy in everything; look forward to what I need to do.
2. **The first 3 miles** ([[Principles/first-3-miles]]) — push past the first 3 miles before flow/the zone starts.
3. **Be in the NOW** ([[Principles/now]]) — worry comes from future/past thinking; nothing is wrong NOW.
4. **No opinion** ([[Principles/no-opinion]]) — as few opinions as needed about everything; the way to peace.

## Top bunch — deep (2026-05-16)

The 4 live, unpacked against the map.

**#3 [[Principles/now]] (hub A) and #4 [[Principles/no-opinion]] (hub B) are the two entry points — and they're the *method*, not the goal.** NOW = where to be; No-opinion/Less-thought = how to get there (stop narrating). They sit on the spine you don't name: reinterpretation ([[Principles/happy-interpretations-minus-expectations]]/[[Principles/context-over-content]]) + surrender (basin D). You reach for the hubs; the spine is what makes them hold. Failure mode: without the spine, "be in the NOW" becomes one more thing to manage.

**#1 "Enjoyment over management" ([[Principles/enjoy]], basin F) is an *application*, not a hub.** Read as one unit: run on enjoyment as the governing mode *instead of* management as the governing mode (management = the self-assigned overhead — worrying, proving, optimising time — per [[Principles/quit-jobs]]/[[Principles/no-obligation]]). It is not two halves; the whole principle is [[Principles/enjoy]]. It rides on 3+4: you can only choose the enjoyment-mode once thinking is quiet (4) and you're in the moment (3). Failure mode: "find joy in *everything*" collides with reality the moment a task genuinely isn't fun.

**#2 [[Principles/first-3-miles]] (60, basin G) is the other application — and it directly contradicts #1.** #1 itself says *it must be enjoyable, look forward to it*. #2 (with [[Principles/seek-discomfort]] 32) says *break through the wall*. The wall is, by definition, the un-enjoyable part. **Your two most-live principles fight each other.** (Note: [[Principles/easy-not-hard]] is *not* part of this — the user marked it standalone 2026-05-16; the tension lives in #1↔#2 directly, not via 28.) Same effort-vs-effortless fault line — now at the very top of your stack.

**The resolution is not a missing fifth principle — it's a sub-principle of #1 you under-reach for (user-stated 2026-05-16).** [[Principles/looking-forward-to-it]] is the operational *child* of [[Principles/enjoy]] (#1) and [[Principles/replace]] — not a top-bunch peer. Replace (basin B) says don't fight the dread, switch the frame; applied to avoidance that *is* "look forward to it"; its output is #1 (invited-hard → enjoyed). So the bunch stays 4. The fix is a move, not a new slot:

> When #1 (enjoy) hits #2's wall and they fight: drop into enjoy's own child — replace the dread-frame, invite the hard onset — and #1 absorbs #2.

Coherent sequence: No-opinion (4, stop narrating the wall as suffering) → **invite it (6 = enjoy's sub-principle via replace)** → break through (2) → land in NOW (3) → enjoy (1).

**Sharpest takeaway:** #1↔#2 only *feels* like a contradiction because you reach for #1 (enjoy) as a flat command and skip its mechanism. "Look forward to it" isn't a separate principle to add to rotation — it's how #1 was always supposed to work. The link was inside #1 the whole time; you stopped descending into it.
